AI Regulation & Constitutional Governance

AI regulation is often discussed after systems are deployed. This track explores what changes if governance comes first.

What do we observe?

  • AI systems are entering public administration, education, work, healthcare, and legal processes.
  • Many systems are hard to audit once integrated.
  • Rules often arrive after technology has already shaped behaviour and expectations.

What tension emerges?

We want innovation, but we also need democratic control, transparency, responsibility, and legal protection. The challenge is not to stop AI. The challenge is to govern it before it becomes structurally unavoidable.

What could change?

Instead of regulating only outputs, a constitutional approach asks what systems may become, who can change them, how decisions are logged, and how errors can be repaired.
