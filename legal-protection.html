Legal Protection & Recoverability

When automated or hybrid systems make mistakes, the central question is not only who is right. It is how a lawful and stable situation can be restored.

What do we observe?

  • People can lose track of complex processes across documents, decisions, agencies, and time.
  • Responsibility can be hard to locate.
  • Errors may be visible only after consequences have already occurred.

What tension emerges?

Digital efficiency can reduce friction for institutions while increasing friction for citizens who must prove what happened.

Alternative direction

A recoverability approach maps events, relationships, documentation, decisions, and consequences.
